If you read something about the election and you feel like sharing it with your friend or family member, take a breath, and think about whether it’s worth having another 30-minute discussion about something you don’t control. You’ve had hundreds of those conversations, probably even more inside your own head, haven’t you? Look at it this way: You probably could have written a book in the time you spent talking, doomscrolling, and mentally spinning out about politics this year. Right now, for most of us, politics consists of a lot of disparate things that we can’t really control anyway. We all want to be happy and live our lives in the best way. But to do that, we have to choose what we do and don’t react to.
